# Rules of Engagement (RoE)

## Learning Objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

- Understand what Rules of Engagement (RoE) are.
- Learn why RoE are essential in ethical hacking.
- Identify the key components of a Rules of Engagement document.
- Understand the responsibilities of both the client and the ethical hacker.
- Recognize the importance of operating within an authorized scope.

---

# Introduction

Before an ethical hacker begins testing a system, both the client and the security team must agree on **how the assessment will be conducted**.

This agreement is called the **Rules of Engagement (RoE)**.

The Rules of Engagement define the scope, objectives, limitations, responsibilities, communication procedures, and legal requirements for a security assessment.

Without clear Rules of Engagement, a penetration test can lead to misunderstandings, unexpected system disruptions, or even legal consequences.

---

# What are Rules of Engagement?

Rules of Engagement (RoE) are a formal agreement between the client and the ethical hacking team that defines how a security assessment will be performed.

They establish clear boundaries and ensure that all testing activities are conducted safely, legally, and professionally.

---

# Definition

> **Rules of Engagement (RoE) are a documented set of guidelines that define the scope, objectives, permissions, limitations, communication procedures, and responsibilities for an authorized security assessment.**

---

# Why are Rules of Engagement Important?

Rules of Engagement help to:

- Ensure legal compliance.
- Protect business operations.
- Prevent misunderstandings.
- Clearly define responsibilities.
- Reduce operational risk.
- Improve communication.
- Ensure testing remains within the approved scope.
- Protect both the client and the ethical hacking team.

---

# Objectives of Rules of Engagement

The primary objectives are to:

- Define the testing scope.
- Establish legal authorization.
- Protect critical business systems.
- Minimize operational disruption.
- Define communication channels.
- Specify reporting requirements.
- Ensure safe security testing.

---

# Key Components of Rules of Engagement

A professional Rules of Engagement document typically includes:

1. Scope
2. Objectives
3. Authorization
4. Timeline
5. Testing Limitations
6. Communication Plan
7. Emergency Contacts
8. Reporting Requirements
9. Confidentiality
10. Success Criteria

---

# 1. Scope

The scope clearly identifies which systems are included and excluded from testing.

## Example

### In Scope

- Corporate website
- Web application
- Internal network
- VPN gateway
- API endpoints

### Out of Scope

- Third-party systems
- Production databases (if excluded)
- Employee personal devices
- Customer systems

Testing outside the approved scope is not permitted.

---

# 2. Objectives

The engagement objectives explain what the organization wants to achieve.

Examples include:

- Identify vulnerabilities.
- Assess security controls.
- Evaluate incident response.
- Test authentication mechanisms.
- Validate security configurations.

---

# 3. Authorization

Authorization confirms that the client has granted permission to perform the assessment.

Authorization should include:

- Client approval
- Written authorization
- Approved testing period
- Authorized testing team
- Client representatives

No testing should begin without authorization.

---

# 4. Timeline

The timeline specifies when testing may occur.

It includes:

- Start date
- End date
- Testing hours
- Maintenance windows
- Reporting deadlines

Scheduling helps reduce the impact on business operations.

---

# 5. Testing Limitations

Certain activities may be restricted to protect systems and users.

Examples include:

- No Denial-of-Service (DoS) testing
- No physical intrusion
- No social engineering (unless approved)
- No password spraying against production accounts
- No destructive testing

Restrictions vary depending on the engagement.

---

# 6. Communication Plan

Clear communication is essential throughout the engagement.

The communication plan defines:

- Primary contacts
- Technical contacts
- Escalation procedures
- Daily status updates
- Emergency notifications

---

# 7. Emergency Contacts

Unexpected situations may occur during testing.

Emergency contacts should include:

- Project Manager
- Security Team Lead
- System Administrator
- Network Administrator
- Executive Contact

These contacts ensure rapid coordination if an issue arises.

---

# 8. Reporting Requirements

The Rules of Engagement should specify how findings will be reported.

Typical deliverables include:

- Executive Summary
- Technical Report
- Vulnerability Details
- Risk Ratings
- Screenshots
- Evidence
- Remediation Recommendations

---

# 9. Confidentiality

Ethical hackers often access sensitive information.

The Rules of Engagement should define:

- Data handling procedures
- Confidentiality requirements
- Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs)
- Report distribution
- Data retention policies

Protecting client information is a professional responsibility.

---

# 10. Success Criteria

The engagement should define what constitutes a successful assessment.

Examples include:

- All agreed systems tested.
- Vulnerabilities documented.
- Reports delivered on time.
- Security recommendations provided.
- Client objectives achieved.

---

# Roles and Responsibilities

## Client Responsibilities

- Provide authorization.
- Define the scope.
- Supply required information.
- Identify critical systems.
- Designate points of contact.
- Review final reports.

---

## Ethical Hacker Responsibilities

- Stay within the approved scope.
- Follow the Rules of Engagement.
- Protect confidential information.
- Document all findings.
- Report vulnerabilities responsibly.
- Avoid unnecessary disruption.

# Best Practices

Always:

- Obtain written authorization.
- Read the Rules of Engagement carefully.
- Stay within scope.
- Maintain communication.
- Document every finding.
- Protect sensitive information.
- Report security issues immediately if critical.

Never:

- Exceed the approved scope.
- Perform unauthorized testing.
- Ignore client restrictions.
- Share confidential information.
- Disrupt production systems unnecessarily.

---

# Real-World Example

A company hires an ethical hacking team to assess its online banking platform.

The Rules of Engagement specify:

- Only the web application may be tested.
- Testing will occur during business hours.
- Denial-of-Service testing is prohibited.
- Critical vulnerabilities must be reported immediately.
- A final report is due within five business days after testing.

By following these rules, the assessment is completed safely, professionally, and without disrupting customer services.

---

# Summary

- Rules of Engagement define how an ethical hacking assessment will be conducted.
- They establish legal authorization, scope, responsibilities, and communication procedures.
- Following the Rules of Engagement protects both the client and the ethical hacker.
- Professional security assessments always begin with a clearly documented Rules of Engagement.
- Staying within the approved scope is one of the most important responsibilities of an ethical hacker.
